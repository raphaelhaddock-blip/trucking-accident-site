## 1. Jackknife Accidents
**Slug:** `jackknife-accidents`
**H1:** Jackknife Truck Accidents
**Primary Keyword:** jackknife accident lawyer

**What It Is:**
When a semi-truck's trailer swings out to form a 90-degree angle with the cab, resembling a folding knife. The truck loses control and often sweeps across multiple lanes.

**Common Causes:**
- Sudden braking on slick roads
- Speeding into curves
- Improper braking technique
- Brake malfunction
- Empty or lightly loaded trailers
- Driver inexperience

**FMCSA Connections:**
- 49 CFR 393 (brake requirements)
- 49 CFR 396 (inspection requirements)
- 49 CFR 392.14 (hazardous conditions driving)

**Typical Injuries:**
- Traumatic brain injuries
- Spinal cord injuries
- Multiple vehicle collision injuries
- Crush injuries

**Content Must Include:**
- How jackknife happens (physics)
- Why empty trailers jackknife more
- Brake system failure analysis
- Multi-vehicle pileup liability
- Evidence from ELD and black box

## 2. Rollover Accidents
**Slug:** `rollover-accidents`
**H1:** 18-Wheeler Rollover Accidents
**Primary Keyword:** semi truck rollover lawyer

**What It Is:**
When a semi-truck tips onto its side or completely flips. Can be tripped (hitting something) or untripped (loss of control).

**Common Causes:**
- Taking curves too fast
- Improperly loaded cargo (high center of gravity)
- Overcorrecting steering
- Wind gusts on empty trailers
- Soft shoulders
- Tire blowouts

**FMCSA Connections:**
- 49 CFR 393.100-136 (cargo securement)
- 49 CFR 392.9 (safe loading)
- Driver training requirements

**Typical Injuries:**
- Fatalities (high percentage)
- Crush injuries
- Ejection injuries
- Burns (fuel spills)

**Content Must Include:**
- Cargo loading standards
- Center of gravity analysis
- Tanker rollover specifics
- Wind speed limitations
- Driver training deficiencies

## 3. Underride Accidents
**Slug:** `underride-accidents`
**H1:** Underride Truck Accidents
**Primary Keyword:** underride accident attorney

**What It Is:**
When a smaller vehicle slides under the trailer of a semi-truck, often shearing off the top of the vehicle. Can be rear or side underride.

**Common Causes:**
- Truck stopping suddenly
- Car failing to stop
- Inadequate underride guards
- Poor lighting on trailer
- Side impacts at intersections

**FMCSA Connections:**
- 49 CFR 393.86 (rear impact guards)
- NHTSA underride guard standards
- Reflective tape requirements (393.11)

**Typical Injuries:**
- Decapitation (often fatal)
- Severe head trauma
- Upper body crushing
- Death

**Content Must Include:**
- Rear vs side underride
- Guard effectiveness data
- Proposed legislation (SITA Act)
- Truck lighting requirements
- Wrongful death claims

## 4. Rear-End Collisions
**Slug:** `rear-end-collisions`
**H1:** Rear-End Truck Collisions
**Primary Keyword:** rear ended by 18 wheeler

**What It Is:**
When an 18-wheeler strikes the rear of another vehicle. Due to stopping distance (300+ feet at highway speed), these are often catastrophic.

**Common Causes:**
- Following too closely
- Distracted driving
- Fatigued driving
- Brake failure
- Wet/icy roads
- Traffic slowdowns

**FMCSA Connections:**
- 49 CFR 395 (Hours of Service)
- 49 CFR 392.3 (fatigued operation)
- Following distance recommendations

**Typical Injuries:**
- Whiplash (severe)
- Spinal injuries
- Crush injuries
- Death in smaller vehicles

**Content Must Include:**
- Stopping distance calculations
- Fatigue as contributing factor
- ELD data analysis
- Speed before impact evidence

### Comparative Negligence Types

**Pure Comparative:**
- Recover damages minus your fault percentage
- Even at 99% fault, recover 1%
- States: California, Florida (changed 2023 to modified), New York

**Modified 50%:**
- Recover if 50% or less at fault
- Barred if 51%+ at fault
- States: Georgia

**Modified 51%:**
- Recover if 51% or less at fault
- Barred if 52%+ at fault
- States: Texas, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Illinois, Indiana, most states

**Contributory:**
- Any fault bars recovery (harsh)
- States: North Carolina, Virginia, Alabama, Maryland, DC
